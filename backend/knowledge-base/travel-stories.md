## Skydiving & solo travels in Europe

I went skydiving in the south of France — a small town called Pau, right near the Spanish border. I did eight jumps total over a few days. All solo — supervised, but solo.
On my fourth jump, I mistimed my flare. That's the maneuver you do right before landing to slow your descent — get it right and you touch down gently, get it wrong and you hit the ground hard. I got it wrong. Broke my leg on landing.
Now, I had a limited window in Pau — just a few more days — and I'd come there specifically to get as many jumps in as possible. So the next morning, I went back up and jumped again. Did four more jumps over the next two days with a broken leg.
After that, I traveled to Spain — changed metros, changed buses, walked around a new country by myself, all on that leg. It wasn't comfortable, but I'd planned the trip and I wasn't going to let an injury rewrite it.


## Travelling in Bali

My wife and I went to Bali for ten days right after I graduated from ISB — the honeymoon we'd been postponing for months.
We started in Ubud, which is the quieter, greener part of Bali — rice terraces, art markets, waterfalls. We rented a scooty and just explored for three days. Did a silver-making workshop, ate way too much nasi goreng, and generally moved at a pace that felt like the opposite of the last twelve months.
From there we took a two-hour boat to Gili Trawangan — this tiny island off the east coast. My wife and I cycled the entire perimeter in about 45 minutes. That's how small it is. No cars, no motorbikes on the island — just bicycles and horse carts. We went snorkeling, swam alongside sea turtles, and visited these underwater statues that someone had sunk to create an artificial reef. It's surreal — you're floating above a sculpture garden in the middle of the ocean.
Then Nusa Penida, which is probably the most stunning place I've ever been. Kelingking Beach looks like it was photoshopped — this massive T-Rex shaped cliff dropping into turquoise water. We also did Diamond Beach and Broken Beach, and stayed overnight at a small resort overlooking the coast.
We ended in Uluwatu — the southern tip of mainland Bali. Watched the kecak fire dance at the temple right at sunset, which is basically a hundred men chanting in a circle while a fire burns in the center and the sun drops into the sea behind them. Spent the last couple of days at beach clubs doing absolutely nothing.
Ten days, four islands, one scooty, zero emails.
